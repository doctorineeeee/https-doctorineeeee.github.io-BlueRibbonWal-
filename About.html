Our Misson

At Blue Ribbon Walk, our mission is to actively work towards the prevention of child abuse and create a world where every child feels safe, loved, and nurtured. We are committed to raising awareness about the prevalence and impact of child abuse while providing support to survivors and their families. Through community engagement, education, and advocacy, we strive to break the cycle of abuse and promote an environment where every child can thrive.

Our Vision

Our vision is a society free from child abuse, where children grow up in an atmosphere of compassion, respect, and understanding. We envision a world where child abuse is not only actively prevented but also acknowledged and addressed promptly, providing comprehensive support to those affected. We aim to build a community that prioritizes the safety and well-being of its children, ensuring they have the opportunity to reach their full potential.

Core Team

Rachel, Founder of Blue Ribbon SG

In November 2008, 9-year-old Sarah was identified as a victim of severe child abuse. Initially unnoticed, the abuse came to light when a concerned teacher noticed behavioral changes in March 2008. Sarah bravely shared her story in October, leading to her official identification as a child abuse victim. Grateful for her foster family, social workers, and therapist, Sarah has rebuilt her life through therapeutic activities like art, music, and equine-assisted therapy. Today, she advocates for other survivors as a peer mentor and support group facilitator, supporting their journey to healing and empowerment.

Ethan, Co-Founder of Blue Ribbon SG

In September 2018, Ethan, a 6-year-old boy, was discovered as a victim of prolonged child abuse. Despite the abuse being hidden, a perceptive school counselor noticed signs of distress in Ethan's behavior in May 2000. Encouraging him to open up, Ethan found the courage to disclose his painful experiences in October, leading to his official recognition as a child abuse victim. Reflecting on his tumultuous journey, Ethan expresses profound gratitude for his caring foster parents, dedicated child protection workers, and his compassionate therapist, who have been instrumental in his recovery and transformation.

Sarah, Event Organizer and support group facilitator

In May 2005, Mia, a 10-year-old girl, was identified as a victim of severe child abuse. Despite the abuse going unnoticed, a perceptive teacher observed changes in Mia's behavior in February 2005. Encouraging her to share her experiences, Mia bravely disclosed the abuse in June, leading to her official recognition as a child abuse victim. Grateful for dedicated child protection workers, and her empathetic counselor, Mia has embarked on a journey of healing and growth. Participating in therapeutic activities like art therapy and outdoor adventures provided by organizations.
